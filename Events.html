Comprehensive Events Management Curriculum

This programme explores the principles and practices of managing medium- and large-scale events, including festivals, conventions, concerts, shows, sporting events, and ceremonies. Emphasis is placed on organization, site preparation, communications, personnel, security, evaluation, and innovation.

EV111
Event Management Planning & Co-ordination
Foundations of event lifecycle: concept development, logistics, timelines, and stakeholder management.
EV112
Presentation Skills
Public speaking, pitching to clients, and commanding the stage as an event professional.
EV113
Conference Planning
Venue selection, speaker management, delegate registration, and programme design.
EV114
Corporate Event Management
Product launches, galas, award ceremonies, board retreats, and shareholder meetings.
EV115
Budgeting

Feedback analysis, ROI reporting, and creating lasting community impact.
Programme Prerequisites

Eligibility & Requirements

  • High school diploma or equivalent (for certificate/diploma)
  • Strong written and verbal communication skills
  • Ability to work under pressure and manage multiple deadlines
  • Personal laptop with access to Microsoft Office / Google Workspace
  • Team-oriented mindset and leadership potential
  • Willingness to work irregular hours (events often occur evenings/weekends)
Certificate (4 months) Diploma (12 months) Advanced Diploma (18 months with practicum)

Programme Highlights

Duration: 4-18 months depending on pathway
Practical immersion: Students plan and execute live events — from concept to teardown — as part of the curriculum.
Industry exposure: Site visits to convention centres, festival grounds, and production houses. Guest lectures by leading event planners.
Capstone project: Design and manage a real event for an external client or charity partner.

Key Competencies Gained

  • ✓ End-to-end event planning and execution
  • ✓ Budget development and sponsor negotiation
  • ✓ Crisis management and on-the-fly problem solving
  • ✓ Vendor and venue contract management
  • ✓ Marketing and ticket sales strategies for maximum attendance
Learning by Doing

Real Events, Real Responsibility

At Orovie, we believe that events management cannot be learned from textbooks alone. Our students gain direct practical experience by organising and staffing actual events — from university galas to community festivals, corporate launches, and wedding expos.

You will work alongside industry mentors, manage budgets, coordinate vendors, and handle last-minute challenges in a safe, supervised environment. By graduation, you will have a portfolio of executed events and the confidence to lead any project.
