Comprehensive Tourism Curriculum

Students are introduced to the major concepts, issues, and theories of tourism and hospitality as an economic sector and topic of academic study. The course covers a broad range of topics related to the travel experience, from business development to the linkages between tourism as an industry and the impacts it has on communities and places. Students also explore sustainability, planning, and ethical responsibility.

TM111
Introduction to Tourism
Foundational concepts, the tourism system, economic significance, and industry stakeholders.
GNS111
Use of English – Library & Study Skills
Academic writing, research methodologies, and professional communication in tourism contexts.
TM112
Nigeria People and Culture
Cultural heritage, ethnic diversity, traditions, and their role in domestic tourism development.
TM113
Planning and Development of Hotel Tourism Attraction
Destination planning, attraction lifecycle, and integrated resort development.
TM114
Organisation and Administration of Hotel and Tourism Centre
Operational structures, governance, and management of tourism facilities.

Environmental impact assessment, carrying capacity, and eco-tourism principles.
IM117

TM118
Accommodation and Maintenance Management

TM119
Zoo and Park Management
Wildlife tourism, conservation ethics, visitor management, and interpretive programming.
TM120
Research Method in Catering and Tourism
Quantitative and qualitative research, data analysis, and market intelligence.
TM121
Tourism Transportation
Air, rail, road, and cruise logistics; transportation economics and sustainability.

Pandemics, natural disasters, political instability, and recovery strategies.
Programme Prerequisites

Eligibility & Requirements

  • High school diploma or equivalent
  • Interest in travel, culture, and global affairs
  • Proficiency in English (additional languages an asset)
  • Basic computer literacy
  • Passion for sustainable and responsible tourism
  • Willingness to engage in field trips and site visits
Diploma (18 months) Bachelor's Degree (36 months) Certificate (6 months)

Programme Highlights

Duration: 18 months (Diploma) | 36 months (Degree with internship)
Practical immersion: Field trips to heritage sites, national parks, and tourism enterprises. Live destination planning projects.
Unique features: Study of African craft art and indigenous tourism; festival and recreation management; zoo and park operations.
Industry connections: Partnerships with tourism boards, tour operators, and conservation authorities.

Key Competencies Gained

  • ✓ Develop and market tourism destinations
  • ✓ Plan sustainable tourism initiatives
  • ✓ Manage visitor experiences and attractions
  • ✓ Navigate tourism policy and regulation
  • ✓ Lead tourism enterprises with ethical responsibility
The Orovie Approach

Tourism as a Force for Good

At Orovie, we believe tourism is not merely an industry — it is a powerful tool for cultural exchange, economic development, and environmental stewardship. Our Tourism Management programme emphasizes sustainability, ethical responsibility, and community benefit.

Students learn to balance the demands of commercial tourism with the imperative to protect natural and cultural heritage. Graduates leave as thoughtful leaders who understand that the future of travel depends on integrity, innovation, and respect for the places and people that host visitors.

Cultural Understanding & Global Citizenship

"Our vision is to create a better world through education, integrity and cultural understanding. We understand the diversity and backgrounds of the various cultures within our industry, but affirm the all-important need for professionalism."
