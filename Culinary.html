Programme Structure

Comprehensive Culinary Curriculum

Our Culinary Arts program integrates theoretical knowledge with intensive hands-on training. Students develop mastery in food science, production techniques, nutrition, hygiene, and management — all essential for a thriving career in the global hospitality industry.

CM111
Introduction to Catering & Home Economics
Foundational principles of catering operations, domestic science, and hospitality economics.
CM112
Computer Science
Digital literacy, inventory software, and technological tools for modern kitchen management.
CM113
Use of English – Library & Study Skills
Academic writing, research methodologies, and professional communication in culinary context.
CM114
Food Science & Technology
Chemical and physical properties of ingredients, food preservation, and innovation in gastronomy.
CM115
Nutrition & Dietetics
Diet planning, therapeutic nutrition, and wellness principles for diverse client needs.
CM116
Food Production Theory & Practice
Culinary techniques: stocks, sauces, butchery, vegetable cookery, and bulk production.
CM117
Food Security
Global food systems, sustainability, and ethical sourcing in hospitality.
CM118
Food Precaution & Management
Risk assessment, cross-contamination prevention, and safety protocols.
CM119
Quality Food Production & Services
Consistency, plating excellence, and service standards in high-volume environments.
CM120
Restaurant Operation Management
Front/back of house coordination, cost control, and guest experience optimization.
CM121
Catering Hygiene & Safety
HACCP principles, sanitation regulations, and workplace safety culture.
CM122
Catering Equipment & System
Kitchen machinery, maintenance, workflow design, and energy efficiency.
CM123
Food Science Theory & Practice (Advanced)
Deep dive into molecular gastronomy, fermentation, and sensory analysis.
CM124
Practical in Catering
Intensive lab: full-course preparation, live buffets, and event catering simulations.
CUL220
Managing Management in Catering Business
Licensing, kitchen planning, pricing strategies, party planning and crowd cooking logistics.
CUL225
Baking & Pastry Arts Fundamentals
Bread, laminated dough, desserts, cost control, and presentation techniques.
Essential Toolkit

Requirements & Materials

  • Professional chef’s knife set (8" chef, paring, serrated)
  • Full chef uniform: jacket, check pants, apron, neckerchief
  • Digital probe thermometer & timer
  • Culinary journal / recipe development notebook
  • Cut-resistant gloves & non-slip clogs
  • Mandoline, microplane, piping bags (for advanced modules)
  • Personal mise en place kit (small containers, markers)
  • Pairing essentials for gastronomy modules
Knife skills kit included Safety gear mandatory Uniform provided by institute

Program Highlights

Duration: 12 months intensive (Certificate) | 24 months Diploma pathway.
Hands-on ratio: 70% practical kitchen training, 30% theory.
Industry exposure: Mandatory externship in partner hotels & restaurants.
Class size: Limited to 16 students per session for personalised mentorship.

Learning Outcomes

  • ✓ Master classic and contemporary cooking techniques
  • ✓ Implement food safety and HACCP systems
  • ✓ Design nutritionally balanced menus for diverse diets
  • ✓ Manage kitchen operations, costing, and procurement
  • ✓ Lead culinary teams with professionalism and creativity
Beyond the classroom

Direct Practical Experience

As emphasised in our institutional philosophy: "A vital aspect of these programs is the opportunity provided for students to gain direct practical experience during the period." Our students train in our industrial-grade demo kitchens, participate in live service events, and complete internships at premium establishments.

You will graduate with a professional portfolio, industry network, and the confidence to excel in competitive culinary environments — from luxury hotels to fine dining restaurants, cruise lines, or your own catering venture.
