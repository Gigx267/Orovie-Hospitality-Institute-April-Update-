Comprehensive Protocol Curriculum

This programme develops students in personal leadership, emotional intelligence, legendary service, titles and forms of address, international protocol, gifts, ceremonies, logistics, and military protocol. Graduates emerge as confident, culturally intelligent professionals equipped for diplomatic, corporate, and luxury service environments.

PEL101
Personal Leadership & Emotional Intelligence
Self-awareness, resilience, empathy, and leading with authenticity in high-stakes settings.
PEL102
Legendary Service: Anticipating the Unspoken
Exceeding expectations, reading guest preferences, and delivering unforgettable experiences.
PEL103
Titles, Forms of Address & Social Hierarchies
Correct usage for royalty, diplomats, officials, and corporate executives globally.
PEL104
International Protocol & Gift-Giving Customs
Cross-cultural nuances, appropriate gifts, and avoiding diplomatic faux pas.
PEL105
Introduction to the Master Protocol Process
Systematic framework for planning and executing protocol-driven events.
PEL106
Flags, Logos, Anthems & National Symbols
Order of precedence, flag etiquette, anthem protocols, and ceremonial usage.
PEL107
Ceremonies, Logistics & Administrative Tools
Seating plans, processions, guest flow, invitations, and run-of-show management.
PEL108
Military Protocol & Personal Diplomacy
Honours, salutes, official visits, and building cross-cultural diplomatic relationships.
PEL201
Dining Etiquette: Global Traditions
American, Continental, Asian, and Middle Eastern dining customs, table settings, and hosting.
PEL202
Business Etiquette & Corporate Protocol
Meeting behaviour, virtual presence, networking, and cross-border business norms.
PEL203
Wardrobe & Grooming for Distinction
Dress codes (white tie, black tie, business formal, smart casual), cultural modesty, and personal presentation.
PEL204
Crisis Communication & Poise Under Pressure
Maintaining composure, managing difficult guests, and reputation protection.
Programme Prerequisites

Eligibility & Requirements

  • High school diploma or equivalent (for certificate)
  • Fluent English (additional languages an asset)
  • Professional demeanour and commitment to refinement
  • Interview with programme director (in-person or virtual)
  • Business formal attire for all practical sessions
  • Openness to cross-cultural learning and international protocols
Certificate (3 months) Advanced Diploma (8 months) Executive Intensive (2 weeks)

Programme Highlights

Duration: 3 months (Certificate) | 8 months (Advanced Diploma with practicum)
Format: Highly interactive workshops, case studies, simulated diplomatic events, and real-world protocol exercises.
Unique features: Live ceremonial simulations, flag and anthem drills, and guest lectures by former diplomats and chief of protocol officers.

Key Competencies Gained

  • ✓ Master international dining and social etiquette
  • ✓ Represent organisations at the highest diplomatic and corporate levels
